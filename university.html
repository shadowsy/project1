UWEC

     The university of wisconsin-eau Claire, formerly known as eau Claire teachers college, later joined the Wisconsin state university system. With an enrollment of 10,500 students, the university offers more than 80 undergraduate programs and 14 master's programs in liberal arts, business, education and humanities, and nursing and health sciences. The university of wisconsin-eau Claire's greatest strength is its emphasis on undergraduate scientific research. Professors often work with students on a variety of research projects, enabling undergraduate students to gain valuable experience that can only be gained by graduate students at larger schools. Students often present their research at regional and national conferences, and many publish their work as undergraduates, some in prestigious journals. Thanks to its reputation for teaching, uw-eau Claire attracts a large number of high-quality Midwestern students and is one of the few regional universities in the United States to have rhodes scholars. Our students often receive Fulbright scholarships and other prestigious scholarships and honors.

SIUE

    Southern Illinois University Edwardsville is a public university in Edwardsville, Illinois.[3] SIUE was established in 1957 as an extension of Southern Illinois University Carbondale.[citation needed] It is the younger of the two major institutions of Southern Illinois University system, and, as of 2018, has the larger enrollment.[4] The University offers graduate programs through its Graduate School. The majority of SIUE's students are from Illinois, with out-of-state and international students accounting for 18% of enrollment.[5] As of 2018, for nearly all of its academic programs, SIUE does not charge out-of-state tuition and fee rates, such that the standard rates are the same for all U.S. residents.[6] The university offers numerous extracurricular activities to its students, including athletics, honor societies, student clubs and organizations, as well as fraternities and sororities. The university has more than 101,000 alumni.[7] Fielding athletic teams known as the SIU Edwardsville Cougars, the university participates in the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) at the Division I level as a member of Ohio Valley Conference (OVC)
